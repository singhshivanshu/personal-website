Speech Processing

  • This project tries to explore the scope of wavelet trnsform in the filled of speech processing &recognition

  • The goal of any speech enhancement system is to get the utmost details out of any given signal while maintaining the quality and/or intelligibility of the speech

  • Motivated by challenging topics of modern speech technology such as speech classification, speech enhancement for hearing aids and noise reduction for speech recognition, wavelet analysis with its technically powerful characteristics is exploited to develop novel methods for improving the performance and robustness of the addressed systems.

  • A wavelet is a wave-like oscillation with an amplitude that begins at zero, increases, and then decreases back to zero. It can typically be visualized as a "brief oscillation" like one recorded by a seismograph or heart monitor.

  • Wavelets are intentionally crafted to have specific properties that make them useful for signal processing. Using a technique called convolution, wavelets can be combined with known portions of a damaged signal to extract information from the unknown portionss
